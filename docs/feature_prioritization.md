# Feature Prioritization

## Prioritization Framework

The MVP uses a practical blend of user pain, business leverage, and implementation complexity.

Scoring lenses:
- analyst and operator time saved
- decision quality improvement
- visibility into ARR and churn risk
- explainability and trust
- implementation effort

## Prioritized Features

| Feature | User Value | Business Value | Build Effort | Priority | Rationale |
|---|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| Ticket classification | High | High | Medium | P0 | Reduces manual triage and powers downstream analysis |
| Executive KPI layer | High | High | Low | P0 | Essential for quick stakeholder adoption |
| Category and segment trend analysis | High | High | Low | P0 | Core analyst workflow |
| Prioritization ranking | High | High | Medium | P0 | Strong bridge from analytics to product decision-making |
| Theme summaries | Medium | High | Medium | P1 | Improves weekly review speed and communication quality |
| Experiment comparison | Medium | Medium | Low | P1 | Valuable for PM and support ops learning loops |
| Export / report handoff | Medium | Medium | Low | P2 | Nice to have after core insight loop works |
| Real-time ingestion | Medium | Medium | High | P3 | Explicitly out of MVP scope |

## Why The MVP Is Sequenced This Way

- Classification is the foundation for consistency.
- Trends and KPI views establish analyst trust.
- Prioritization converts descriptive analytics into action.
- Summaries and experiment views strengthen cross-functional adoption.
